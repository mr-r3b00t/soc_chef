Intrusion Isolation Strategy for Top Cyber Incident Scenarios

Scenario Device Category Isolation Strategy Rationale, Scope, and Justification Isolate Entire Network Rationale for Network Isolation Choice
Phishing Attack Leading to Credential Theft Managed PC
  • Isolate affected user's device via EDR, restricting both ingress and egress.
  • Reset compromised credentials and enforce MFA.
  • Scan device for malware and indicators of compromise (IOCs).
  • Monitor network for lateral movement attempts.
Rationale, Scope, and Justification

Rationale: Phishing attacks often lead to credential theft, enabling unauthorized access. Isolating the device prevents further exploitation, while credential reset and MFA mitigate ongoing risks.

Scope: Limited to the affected device and user account.

Justification: Targeted isolation minimizes disruption while addressing the breach effectively.

 No

Rationale: The threat is contained to a single device and user, not requiring network-wide isolation.

Scope: Device-specific containment.

Justification: Proportional response avoids unnecessary network impact.
Phishing Attack Leading to Credential Theft Unmanaged PC
  • Block device IP/MAC from network access, restricting both ingress and egress.
  • Reset compromised credentials and enforce MFA.
  • Notify user to secure their device.
  • Monitor network for lateral movement attempts.
Rationale, Scope, and Justification

Rationale: Unmanaged devices lack direct control, necessitating network-level blocking to halt threats. Credential reset and MFA prevent further misuse.

Scope: Limited to the affected device and user account.

Justification: Targeted measures suffice for a single-device compromise.

 No

Rationale: Containment at the device level prevents escalation without network-wide measures.

Scope: Device-specific containment.

Justification: Efficient and proportionate response.

Scope: Not applicable.

Ransomware Infection via Exploit Kit Managed PC
  • Isolate infected device via EDR, restricting both ingress and egress.
  • Quarantine device in a VLAN with no external access.
  • Scan network for similar IOCs.
  • Implement network segmentation.
Rationale, Scope, and Justification

Rationale: Ransomware spreads quickly; isolation and quarantine prevent further encryption and communication. Scanning and segmentation limit impact.

Scope: Infected device and related network segments.

Justification: Balances containment with minimal disruption.

 Deny Egress from all PCs

Rationale: Prevents ransomware from contacting C2 servers or exfiltrating data across all PCs.

Scope: All PCs, managed and unmanaged.

Justification: Broad egress denial mitigates undetected spread.
Ransomware Infection via Exploit Kit Unmanaged PC
  • Block infected device by MAC/IP, restricting both ingress and egress.
  • Quarantine device in a restricted VLAN.
  • Broadcast user notifications to disconnect devices.
  • Implement temporary egress filtering.
Rationale, Scope, and Justification

Rationale: Network-level blocking and quarantine contain ransomware on unmanaged devices. Notifications and filtering enhance protection.

Scope: Infected device and temporary network measures.

Justification: Effective containment with limited scope.

 Deny Egress from all PCs

Rationale: Blocks C2 communication and data exfiltration across all PCs.

Scope: All PCs, managed and unmanaged.

Justification: Prevents escalation from unmanaged devices.
